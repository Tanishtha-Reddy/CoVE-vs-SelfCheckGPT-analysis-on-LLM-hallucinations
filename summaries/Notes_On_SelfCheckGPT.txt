1. Main Idea:
	- Main intention is to help reduce hallucinations (wrong information) in LLMs.
	- SelfCheckGPT is a type of method to fact-check answers of balck-box models (models whose internal functions are a mystery to users) without any external database.
	- SelfCheckGPT is a sampling-based apprach that can detect whether responses generated by LLMs are hallucinated or factual.
	- Claim that their approach gives better/higher AUC-PR scores when compared to other baselines.
	-
2.How it works (with BERTScore):
	i.   Give the LLM a  prompt. The Answer it generates is OG(original) answer.
	ii.  Give the LLM the same prompt multiple times in a row. Each answer is Ans1, Ans2, ... AnsN.
	iii. Break OG answer, usually into several sentences.
	iv.  Compare the broken OG answer with each AnsN.
	v.   BERTscore measures how similar they are in meaning & structure.
	vi.  Find most similar sentence in each of the sentences:
		If high score = most likely factual/ real information.
		If low score  = most likely a hallucination.
	vii. For each sentence, we get a hallucinatin score (between 0 and 1):
		If closer to 0 = most likely hallucinated.
		If closer to 1 = most likely factual data.
3.Key Findings:
	 - This method is high in speed, low in memory usage, and gives high computational efficiency.
4.Limitations:
	 - This goes sentence level checking, but sentences may contain both factual and not-factual information.
