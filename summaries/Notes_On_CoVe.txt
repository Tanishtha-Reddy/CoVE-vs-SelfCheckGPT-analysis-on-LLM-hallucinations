1. Main Idea:
	- Main intention is to correct hallucinatins generated by LLMs using Chain-of-Verification(CoVe) method.
	- CoVe is a method to self-verify answers in LLMs.
	- CoVe's key assumption is that an LLM when prompted can execute a plan on how to verify itself to check its own work and then rpovide an improved response.
	- They find that independant verification questions tend to give better answers than OG (original) answers, and hence improve the correctness of the overall response.

2.How it works:
	i.   Give the LLM a  prompt. The Answer it generates is OG(original) answer i.e, you get a baseline response.
	ii.  Ask the LLM to generate 10 verification questions based on the promt and its answer.
	iii. Without showing the LLM its baseline answer, ask each of the verification answers independantly/seperately.
	iv.  Now, ask the LLM the orginal prompt.

3.Key Findings:
	 - CoVe improves performance in list-based answer tasks, closed book QA, and longform generation.
	 - Instruction-tuning and Chain-of-Thought (CoT) do not reduce hallucinations.
	 - Independant verification questions tend to provide better results than the original longform answer.
4.Limitations:
	 - Does not completely eliminate hallucinations.
	 - High computational cost.
